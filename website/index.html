Binnovative Boston

Binnovative is a unique non-profit organization that endeavors to bring together the entrepreneurial communities in Japan and the US in a spirit of collaboration and mutual benefit. Binnovative Boston is the Boston chapter of Binnovative and contributes to the entrepreneurial ecosystem of Boston by organizing various collaborative events with cities in Japan.

Kojiya

Kojiya is a leading producer of koji (a traditional Japanese food additive) in Japan and has been in business since 1689 (for 326 years). It plans to introduce its products in the US market. Through the Business Plan Challenge, Kojiya is aiming to gain deep insight into the US market and to seek innovative and practical recommendations for its US marketing plan. Kojiya’s president, Myoho Asari, is flying in from Oita, Japan especially for this event.

As a participant, you will have the opportunity to work in teams with university students from Japan. You will have the opportunity to directly interact with Ms. Asari – a pioneer in her field. This is a great opportunity to gain real international experience while researching the US and Japanese markets, experimenting with Kojiya’s products, and receiving hands-on mentoring from experienced professionals in Boston.

Recommendations made by the teams in this competition will be incorporated into Kojiya’s US marketing plan. This is a great opportunity to make a real positive impact on Kojiya.

Participation is not limited to students of business or economics; students of all backgrounds are welcomed. Proficiency in Japanese is not a requirement for participation.

What is Koji (糀)?

Koji is steamed rice that has been inoculated with koji spores (Aspergillus orzyae) and incubated to allow the fungus to grow. Rich in enzymes, koji is the key ingredient in all the basic foods of the traditional Japanese diet. It is used as a starter to make miso, soy sauce, sake, shochu, mirin and vinegar.

It is produced from a traditional and natural fermentation process. It contains enzymes to break down proteins to produce amino acids including glutamate.

It also adds "umami" punch to vegetables and fish and tenderizes meat. It helps to reduce the use of salt, sugar and fat.
